Bengaluru Local

In a recent media conference respected Minister of higher education Dr. Ashwath Narayan C N said that the semester end examination of students pursuing their engineering in semester 2 has been cancelled.They will be promoted to semester 3.They will be awarded their grades and marks on the basis of internal assessment. He also added by saying that Karnataka Education Dept is looking forward to implement like that of Maharashtra Govt. Students studying technical degree shall be promoted to next year of their respective degree.The minister also added that the UGC's directions will be ammended once we discuss it with them.I hope every state in the country will move with this decission. An official said that as the pandemic is increasing day-by-day, the colleges can't complete the prescribed syllabus efficiently. Even if the lockdown eases the colleges shall re-open by August. But students should not just stream amazonPrime and Netflix in their home,they should also look forward for some additional knowledge. Official said that 'We believe in students. They should remember their moral ethics.Should not copy or perform such unethical practices as exams will be online.' Minister stated that engineering students will be promoted to their next sem on the basis of their scores of internal assessment,which will be conducted through online. Every college can either conduct the exam through online or by sending question papers to them personally. Different sets of question paper is expected. He added that a committee will be set up in which teaching faculty of top colleges like RVCE,PESU,BMSCE,DSCE etc. Their suggestions will be considered.
Points that minister Dr. Ashwath Narayan C N asked students to follow up:
1. The respective college shall conduct the internal examination online.
2. 50% of the internal assessment marks will be considered.
3. Another 50% of the marks will be considered on the basis of the quiz,projects etc.
4. Attendance will also be considered while awarding marks and grades to students.
5. He also students to not to get panicked in this situation. He asked the students to contact their academic faculty for any queries.
6. He asked the students to take these internal exams seriously as they have huge weightage.
